Customer Experience Improvement

05 May, 2017 | Carina Chen | Customer Service

Navitas Professional Services has been delivering career readiness solutions to tertiary students, graduates and young professionals since 2008. With the rapid development of the society and the IT area, it seems that lots of complaints have appeared relating to a variety of issues. Therefore, for years, Navitas has always been dedicated to bridge that gap between the young IT professionals and the companies.

1. Graduates lack of culture awareness

2. Graduates lack of knowledge of WHS

3. The format of the assignment

4. The resources available for enrolled students

5. The customer service for potential students

All the above issues will be elaborated in the followings.

In terms of employers in the field of IT, they claimed that the graduates do not have enough soft skills to work in Australia, such as lack of culture awareness in Australia, and lack of the knowledge related with workplace health and safety (WHS). For Navitas, it should bring more knowledge related to local culture throughout the training progress. Moreover, the awareness of WHS can be cultured through continuous training so that its graduates could be competent enough to meet that requirements.

In terms of enrolled students, the problem bothers them most is the assignment. The required format of the assignments sometimes is not reasonable enough. For instance, forcing to use a specific tool (e.g. Google Slides) to present the information, instead of letting students explore the presentation tools by themselves. As a result, students might miss the opportunity to learn other tools (e.g. Prezi). Thus, if Navitas could change the assignment requirements for students, then that annoyance might be avoided.

Besides, it seems to be a common problem that there has not sufficient rooms for students to perform their assigned activities. For the purpose of better development, Navitas should aim at removing those limitations and doing everything it can to improve the resources available to its students.

Regarding the potential students, lots of complaints are focused on the services they received from the reception or the hotline of Navitas, as can be easily found online. To be more specific, it is described as the worst customer experience ever. In order to solve that problem, Navitas should provide better service training classes to its receptionists so that an excellent customer service could be offered.
